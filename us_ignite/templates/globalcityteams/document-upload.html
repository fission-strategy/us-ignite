Submitting a One-Page Summary of Your Contributions to a Potential Project

A key objective of the Challenge is to match interested parties to projects that will help accelerate the effective deployment of Internet of Things (IoT) and Cyber Physical Systems (CPS) technologies, products and solutions in Smart City/Community environment.

To match interested parties to each other and to projects, we are asking for your help. Specifically, we are asking you to provide a one-to-two page description (hereafter “one-pager”) of the contributions that you bring to a potential project - your piece of the puzzle.

What will NIST and US IGNITE do with the submissions?  

NIST and US Ignite will post all the one-pagers that we receive to an online repository which will be be open to any interested party to review. Wherever possible, we will seek to organize into “action clusters” submissions that express interest in similar categories of IoT/CPS application. We expect the “action clusters” that you help identify will help us organize the kick-off workshop (September 29-30 in Gaithersburg, MD – please be sure to register here).

Additionally, please note that submitting a one-pager will indicate to NIST and US Ignite that we can contact you in order to discuss in more detail your involvement in a potential Global City Teams Challenge project.

What information should I include in my one-pager?  

To facilitate review of the submissions, we request that interested parties submit a one-pager that addresses the items listed below. To the extent possible, please be thorough with your answers, but please refrain from revealing proprietary information as the contents of your information will be publicly available.   

Please include:

  1. Contact Details: Name, email, and web site link (if applicable).
  2. Organization Details: Brief description of your organization or participating team
    1. As a technology researcher or solutions provider, please describe the features, functionality, components, and anticipated users of the IoT/CPS technology, product and solution that you currently operate.
    2. Describe, if any, the existing interface for connecting to and exchanging data with other devices/products/solutions. Please include sufficient detail for the organizers to understand its capabilities. (Links to FAQs work as well)
    3. As a community, what infrastructure is available in order to test, develop or deploy an IoT application? What challenge(s) do you seek to address using an IoT deployment?
  3. The Application Use Case or Scenario: Please describe your proposed scenario for integrated IoT/CPS solutions. Please provide sufficient detail to explain how the proposed solutions would create and demonstrate systems that work together in smart city/community environment. Also indicate the types of partners that would be involved.
  4. Key Benefits: What important benefits would result from this system, if demonstrated and ultimately implemented? This should include economic benefits (new businesses, jobs, economic development, exports) as well as impact on energy, health, safety, environment, and other quality of life aspects.

Need more ideas about what to include in your one-pager?

Community Leaders, Researchers & Entrepreneurs – What application are you most interested seeing in your smarter city? What fundamental infrastructure needs to be in place for you to develop your application? What applications do you think would bring higher quality of life or an improved business environment? What’s on the top of your wish list?

Some examples:

  • Integrated eldercare monitoring & emergency responses systems
  • More efficient weather-port-customs-transportation logistics tools
  • Driverless car readiness requirements
  • High-resolution air and noise quality sensor network for citizens and researchers
  • Smart parking- parking lots paved with web connected, sensor based solar panels

Technologists, Innovators and Developers – Where do you see the most promise by data-sharing and mashups with other technologies, products and solutions? Propose technologies and ideas for deployment of interconnected IoT/CPS solutions in smart cities and communities.
